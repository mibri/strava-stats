## Nutrition — July 9 → race day (assumes ~77 kg; adjust if off)

**Now → 7/12 (full training):** eat normally, don't restrict. Carbs ~5–6 g/kg (~400–450 g) on run days, protein ~1.6–2 g/kg (~125–155 g) daily. Day before Sunday's long run: carb-forward dinner. This run is also the fueling dress rehearsal — compressed race morning (small breakfast 90 min before), 60–80 g carbs/hr during.

**Taper (7/13–7/23):** appetite stays but burn drops (~4,300 → ~2,600 kcal/wk of running). Trim calories slightly via fat/snacks — NOT carbs or protein. Do not try to cut weight. Expect +1–3 lb late in taper: that's stored glycogen + water (each gram of glycogen binds ~3 g water). That weight IS the fuel — don't diet it off. Alcohol minimal, iron/veg normal until 7/24.

**Carb load (Fri 7/24 – Sat 7/25):** 8–10 g/kg/day ≈ 600–750 g carbs/day. That's deliberate work: white rice, pasta, bread, bagels, bananas, juice, sports drink, low-fiber cereal. Go LOW fiber, low fat, modest protein. Salt food generously + drink to thirst. You should feel full and slightly heavy Saturday night — that's the plan working.

**Race morning (5 AM start — compressed protocol):** No 2 AM wake-up needed. The night before does the heavy lifting: early carb-heavy dinner (~6–7 PM) + a pre-bed carb snack ~9 PM (toast w/ honey, banana). Wake ~3:15–3:30 (needed for logistics anyway) and immediately eat a SMALLER breakfast — ~60–80 g of simple, liquid-leaning carbs 90 min out: half a bagel + banana + bottle of sports drink, or similar. Low fiber/fat digests fine in 90 min at that size. Coffee if habitual. Gel 10–15 min before the gun; first on-course gel at mile 3 closes the gap. **Rehearse this exact wake-eat-run sequence on 7/12 and 7/19.**

**During:** 60–80 g/hr — gel (GU) every 25–30 min from mile 3–4, washed down from the 415 ml flask. **Sodium (confirmed salty sweater — hat crust): target ~500–700 mg/hr.** One Liquid IV stick in the flask at the start; carry a second stick dry and add it at a mid-race water-stop refill. Do NOT double-concentrate the small bottle (hypertonic + gels = GI trouble). Course water as backup; Skratch lemon-lime on course if tested 7/20.

## Strength & prehab — arches + hips

**Daily foot circuit (~10 min, through 7/20; every other day race week):**
1. **Short-foot / arch doming** — dome the arch without curling toes, hold 5 s. 2×10 per foot. Start seated; progress to standing, then single-leg stance.
2. **Toe yoga** — lift the big toe with the other four down, then reverse. 2×10 per foot. (Towel scrunches are an acceptable substitute.)
3. **Slow single-leg calf raises** — 3 s down, arch actively engaged, 3×10–12 per side. Add a bent-knee set for the soleus when it feels easy.
4. **Single-leg balance** — 30–45 s per side; progress to eyes closed. Doubles as ankle/ITB-chain stability.
5. **Plantar ball roll** — 1–2 min per foot, evenings. This one is allowed any day, including race eve.

**Hip circuit (3×/week — e.g. Mon/Wed/Fri, ~10 min):** keep the PT clamshell prescription exactly as given, plus side plank w/ top-leg lift 2×20–30 s, single-leg glute bridge 2×10/side, lateral band walks 2×10 steps each way. Weak glute med → knee dives in → arch collapses downstream; this is the same chain that produced the old ITB trouble, so this circuit is race insurance twice over.

**Dosing rules:** start light this week — the goal is learned movements now, maintenance later. Last full hip session **Wed 7/22**; race week foot work every other day, light. Nothing but the ball roll on 7/24–7/25. Any exercise that causes next-day soreness gets halved, not pushed through.
